Change In
Power

Throughout Macbeth power changes hands constantly, from Macbethâ€™s ever increasing control of power to the loss of power shown in Duncan and his sons, these changes in power can have very apparent and quick effects towards each character in the play.

Act I

Macbeth is awarded the Thane of Cawdor by Duncan, the King of Scotland. This begins his ambitious journey for total power. The witches prophecise that Macbeth will become King, as well as the Thane of Cawdor and Glamis. And later in Act 1 Scene IV Macbeth is awarded the Thane of Glamis confirming the witches prophecy.

Act II

Duncan attends a dinner at Macbeth's castle and Macbeth is convinced by Lady Macbeth to murder the King as he would recieve a greater level of power. Macbeth commits to the murder of Duncan and is now next in line to become King as Duncan's sons have fled to England and Ireland.

Act III

Macbeth plots to kill Fleance and Banquo as he believes that they know that Macbeth killed the King and Macbeth is willing to murder his own friend to stay in power. During Act III Scene III, Macbeth commits the murder of banquo however Fleance flees.

Act IV

Macbeth's family is murdered as Macduff has become aware of what Macbeth has done and as such Macbeth believes it is his only option to murder Macduff's family to remain as King of Scotland, however Macbeth failed to murder Macduff. This is the peak of Macbeth's power throughout the play.

Act V

Macbeth is finally confronted for all his wrongdoings and in Act 5 Scene VIII he is killed by Macduff leaving him with no power to him or his heir.
