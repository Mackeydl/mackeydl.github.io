The Effect Of
Power

Many characters in Macbeth were impacted by power throughout the play from the tragic death of Lady Macbeth to the thoughtout death of the King. Each character throughout the play are affected differently in each scenario.

Lady Macbeth

Lady Macbeth has a very noticeable effect on the play throughout her abusive relationship with power. She is first exposed to power through the witches’ words to Macbeth, and soon after her corrupting pursuit of power through Macbeth begins and eventually ends with her death after going insane from the guilt she has received from pursuing Macbeth’s power throughout the play. Her guilt from pursuing Macbeth’s power is shown through “The thane of Fife had a wife. Where is she now?—What, will these hands ne'er be clean?— No more o' that, my lord, no more o' that. You mar all with this starting.”, where Lady Macbeth is unsure if her bloody hands will ever be clean as the blood is used to represent her guilt caused by pursuing Macbeth’s power.

Duncan

During the early stages of Macbeth, Duncan is in full control of all power in Scotland and through Macbeth and Lady Macbeth’s pursuit of power he is killed to allow Macbeth to obtain the divine power of Kings. This is especially pivotal as the killing of Duncan had a drastic effect on the play and had the same effect on Macbeth’s pursuit of power as because of Duncan's murder, other involuntary actions had to be taken to allow Macbeth to continue with his newly found power, most notable the murder of Banquo and Macduff’s family.

Banquo

Banquo is also very seriously impacted by Macbeth and Lady Macbeth’s pursuit of power as initially, Banquo wanted the same power obtained by Macbeth as the Thane of Cawdor but soon realises the effect it has had on Macbeth. Later on in the play, Banquo must pay the ultimate price as he is next in line to become King and have the divine and ultimate power they obtain. This is because Macbeth is required to betray and kill him, so that Macbeth can take Banquo’s position and take the power of Duncan after he is murdered.
